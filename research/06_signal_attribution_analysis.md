\# Signal Attribution Analysis



\## Hypothesis



Portfolio performance is driven by a subset of assets that contribute disproportionately to returns.



\## Methodology



Each asset's contribution to portfolio performance was calculated by combining historical weights with realized future returns.



Total contribution, average weight, and holding frequency were analyzed.



\## Results



GLD, AAPL, USO, and QQQ were the largest positive contributors to portfolio performance. XLV, TLT, and IWM contributed negatively.



Performance was concentrated among a small group of strong momentum winners rather than being evenly distributed across all holdings.



\## Conclusion



The strategy's success is driven by its ability to identify and allocate capital toward a small number of high-conviction momentum opportunities.

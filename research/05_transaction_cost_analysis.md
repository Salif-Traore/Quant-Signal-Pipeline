\# Transaction Cost Analysis



\## Hypothesis



A robust strategy should remain profitable after incorporating realistic transaction costs.



\## Methodology



Transaction costs of 0, 5, 10, and 25 basis points were applied to all portfolio trades.



Performance metrics were recalculated after accounting for turnover-related costs.



\## Results



Returns declined as transaction costs increased. However, the strategy remained profitable and maintained a positive Sharpe ratio even at 10 basis points of trading costs.



\## Conclusion



The strategy appears reasonably robust to moderate transaction costs and is not dependent on unrealistic assumptions of frictionless trading.

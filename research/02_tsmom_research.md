# Time-Series Momentum Research



## Hypothesis



Assets with positive historical returns should continue trending higher, while assets with negative historical returns should underperform.



## Methodology



A time-series momentum strategy was tested using multiple lookback periods, including 1-month, 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month momentum signals.



Assets were held only when momentum exceeded a specified threshold.



## Results



The pure time-series momentum strategy underperformed the existing cross-sectional momentum strategy across multiple performance metrics.



Walk-forward testing showed weaker consistency and lower Sharpe ratios than the production strategy.



## Conclusion



Time-series momentum was not adopted as the primary strategy. However, it remained useful as a research tool and potential filter.

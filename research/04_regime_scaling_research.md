\# Regime Scaling Research



\## Hypothesis



Portfolio exposure should decrease during weaker market environments and increase during stronger market environments.



\## Methodology



Momentum breadth was used as a proxy for market strength. Portfolio exposure was dynamically scaled between minimum and maximum exposure levels.



Several minimum exposure levels were tested including 0.60, 0.70, 0.80, 0.90, and 1.00.



\## Results



Regime scaling reduced volatility and drawdowns. However, walk-forward testing showed that lower exposure levels also reduced returns and Sharpe ratios.



The production strategy maintained the highest walk-forward Sharpe ratio.



\## Conclusion



Regime scaling improved downside protection but did not outperform the fully invested production strategy on a risk-adjusted basis.
